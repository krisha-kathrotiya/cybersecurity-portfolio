# Evidence Notes – Botium Toys Audit

This document summarizes the key findings and evidence collected during the internal security audit of Botium Toys.


## 1. Access Control
- All employees currently have access to sensitive internal data.
- No proper implementation of least privilege.
- No separation of duties in place.

**Risk:** Too many users have unrestricted access, increasing the chance of misuse or data breaches.

## 2. Data Protection
- Credit card data is stored without encryption.
- Weak password policy.
- No password management system implemented.

**Risk:** Sensitive data is highly exposed and vulnerable to unauthorized access.


## 3. Monitoring & Detection
- No Intrusion Detection System (IDS) is implemented.
- Antivirus software is present but has limited detection capability.

**Risk:** Security threats may go undetected, especially advanced or internal attacks.


## 4. Recovery & Continuity
- No data backups are available.
- No disaster recovery plan is defined.

**Risk:** Data loss or system failure could result in permanent damage with no recovery options.


## 5. Physical Security
- Locks, CCTV cameras, and fire protection systems are implemented.
- All physical security systems are operational.

**Status:** Basic physical security measures are properly in place.


## 6. Legacy System Risk
- Systems are monitored manually.
- No defined maintenance or update schedule.

**Risk:** Outdated systems and human error increase overall security vulnerabilities.

## Summary
The audit identified several critical gaps in access control, data protection, monitoring, and recovery processes. Immediate attention is required to reduce risks and improve overall security posture.
